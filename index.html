What is One Act Play?

Every year in Texas since 1927, thousands of high school theatre departments compete in One Act Play (OAP), the worldâ€™s largest play festival of its kind. Months of intense preparation lead to a succession of four sudden death competitions governed by a strict rule book - the two best troupes move on and the rest go home, the stakes raising with each round until the state championship in Austin. Among many other rules, no play can go over forty minutes, and each school gets seven minutes to construct and take down their set at any given competition.

The Film.

The Show Must Go On presents an inside look at the drama that unfolds between high school theatre directors and their ensembles as they travel across Texas for this elimination style tournament. We are following several schools through the auditions, rehearsals, and competitions that make up a season of OAP.
